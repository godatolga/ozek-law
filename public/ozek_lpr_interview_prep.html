Interview Tips & Guidance

From the desk of Tolga Ozek, LL.M., Esq. — what to know before you walk in.

⚠️
Important: Always consult with Ozek Law Firm before your interview. Call +1 (202) 854-8545.
🤝
Be Consistent With Your Spouse
The officer may interview you and your spouse separately. Your answers about home life, daily routines, and your relationship must match. Practice together until your stories align naturally.
📝
Answer Only What Is Asked
Do not volunteer extra information. Answer concisely and directly. If you do not understand a question, politely ask for clarification. You may say "I don't remember" if you genuinely do not.
📁
Organize Your Documents
Arrive with documents organized in the same order as the checklist. Use labeled folders or tabs. Bring originals AND one clean copy of each. All non-English documents need certified translations.
🚫
Never Lie to a Federal Officer
Misrepresentation to USCIS is a federal offense and a permanent bar to immigration benefits. If a question is sensitive, tell your attorney beforehand so you can prepare an honest, strategic answer.
⏰
Arrive Early — Always
Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your appointment. Security lines at USCIS offices can be long. Being late can result in your case being marked as abandoned.
👔
Dress Professionally
First impressions matter. Dress as you would for a job interview — business casual or formal. Avoid casual clothing or anything that may appear disrespectful in a federal setting.
🇹🇷
Turkish-Origin Clients: Special Note
Turkish birth registration and nufus documents require careful preparation. Please inform Ozek Law Firm of your specific citizenship and registration status for correct documentation guidance.
What Happens at the Interview?
1
Check-In
Present your interview notice and ID at security. Wait to be called by an officer.
2
Oath
You will be placed under oath. Everything you say must be truthful.
3
Document Review
The officer reviews your file and the supporting documents you brought.
4
Q&A
You answer questions about your relationship, background, and application.
5
Decision
Approval, RFE, or denial. Contact Ozek Law Firm immediately if anything other than approval occurs.

Medical clearance is valid for 6 months (3 months for certain TB classifications). If you do not travel within the clearance period, you must restart the entire medical process. Do NOT open the sealed results envelope — bring it intact to your interview.
Key Consular Reminders
📮
Sealed Envelope
Do NOT open your medical results envelope. Carry your X-rays with you when you travel to the U.S. for the first time.
💳
USCIS Immigrant Fee
Pay the USCIS Immigrant Fee online before traveling to the U.S. This covers processing of your Permanent Resident Card.
✈️
Travel Before Visa Expires
You must enter the U.S. before your visa expiration date (usually 6 months from issuance). Visas cannot be extended.
🦾
Do Not Make Irreversible Plans
Do not resign from your job, sell property, or book non-refundable flights until your immigrant visa is approved and in hand.
