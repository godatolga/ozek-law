Legal Requirements — Know Your Case

The consular officer will assess each element below. Know how you satisfy each one.

💱 E-1 Treaty Trader — Four Required Elements
1
Treaty Nationality
You are a national of a country with a qualifying Treaty of Commerce and Navigation with the United States.
2
Substantial Trade
A continuous flow of qualifying trade — not a single transaction. Sufficient in volume and value to justify your U.S. presence.
3
Principally with the U.S. (51% Rule)
More than 50% of your total international trade (outside your home country) must be with the United States.
4
Executive, Supervisory or Essential Skills
Coming in an executive or supervisory capacity, or possessing essential skills indispensable to the enterprise.
📊
Know Your Trade Numbers
Know your exact U.S. trade volume and the percentage of your total international trade. Be consistent with the financials in your package.
🤝
"Substantial" Is Relative
"Substantial trade" has no dollar threshold — it refers to a continuous, active exchange of services or goods. Describe the nature, frequency, and volume of your U.S. transactions.
📋
Study the Package
Know the table of contents. Direct the officer to any document within seconds. Re-read the cover letter the night before your interview.
💰 E-2 Treaty Investor — Five Required Elements
1
Treaty Nationality
You are a national of a country maintaining a qualifying treaty with the United States.
2
Substantial Investment
Proportionally significant relative to the total cost of the enterprise. Funds must be "at risk" — committed to the business.
3
Non-Marginal Enterprise
The business must generate significantly more income than needed to support the investor — creating economic benefit, typically through U.S. job creation.
4
Developing & Directing
You must come to direct and develop the enterprise. If you own 50%+, your role must be executive or supervisory.
5
Executive, Supervisory or Essential Skills
Your role is managerial or supervisory, or your specialized skills are essential and not readily available in the U.S. market.
💵
Know Your Exact Investment
Know your investment amount to the dollar and what each portion was spent on. Be ready to walk the officer through the expenditure breakdown.
🔍
Trace the Source of Funds
Know where your capital came from and how it was transferred to the U.S. entity. The complete funds trace is in your package.
📈
5-Year Projections Matter
Know your Year 1, Year 3, and Year 5 revenue targets. Know how many U.S. employees you plan to hire and in what roles.

⚠ Nonimmigrant Intent — Critical for Both Visa Types

The E-1 and E-2 are nonimmigrant visas. You must affirmatively demonstrate your intention to depart when your status ends.

🚨
The officer will be watching for any sign of immigrant intent. Any answer suggesting permanent stay can result in a denial.
🏠
Home Country Ties
Describe concrete ties: property owned or leased, family remaining in the home country, other business interests, financial accounts and assets. Be specific.
📃
Statement of Intent to Depart
Your package includes a signed Statement of Intent to Depart. Know that you signed it. Confirm clearly: "I understand the E-[1/2] is nonimmigrant status and I intend to depart when my status concludes."
🚫
Never Suggest Permanent Intent
Do not say "I plan to stay as long as possible" or "I hope to get a green card." If you have a pending immigrant petition, disclose it to Ozek Law Firm before the interview.
⌨️
Prior U.S. Immigration History
Disclose prior visa denials, overstays, removal proceedings, and pending petitions truthfully. Omissions are treated as misrepresentation.

Interview Tips & Strategy

From the desk of Tolga Ozek, Esq. — what to know, how to present, and how to handle the unexpected.

⚠️
Important: Always consult with Ozek Law Firm before your interview. Call +1 (202) 854-8545.
📊
Know Your Numbers Cold
E-1: exact U.S. trade volume and percentage of international trade. E-2: exact investment amount, breakdown, and 5-year projections. These must come out without hesitation.
🗣️
Answer Only What Is Asked
Do not volunteer extra information. Answer concisely and stop. Extra information creates extra questions.
👔
Dress for Business
Dress as if meeting a bank loan officer — conservative business attire. Looking like a serious businessperson matters from the moment you walk in.
🚫
Never Misrepresent — Ever
Misrepresentation is a permanent bar to U.S. immigration benefits under INA 212(a)(6)(C). Discuss sensitive facts with Ozek Law Firm first.
⏰
Arrive No More Than 30 Minutes Early
Most consulates will not admit you more than 30 minutes before your appointment. Being late can result in your interview being forfeited.
📋
Know Your Package
Study the table of contents. Find any document within seconds and direct the officer to the correct tab immediately.
💻
No Laptops or Large Electronics
Most consulates do not permit laptops, tablets, or backpacks. They will be confiscated. Bring only your documents, photo ID, and MRV fee receipt.
✍️
Write It Down Immediately After
Write down everything you remember the moment you leave the consulate. Contact Ozek Law Firm immediately if anything went wrong: 202.854.8545.
What to Expect at the Consulate
1
Security
Airport-style X-ray. Electronics may be collected. No backpacks.
2
Pre-Interview Check
Sorted by application type. If unsure where to go, ask staff.
3
The Interview
Typically 5-30 minutes. A strong package often means a shorter interview.
4
Decision
Approval, 221(g) request, or denial. A 221(g) is NOT a denial.
5
221(g) — Do Not Panic
Request for more documents or processing. Call Ozek Law Firm immediately: 202.854.8545.
