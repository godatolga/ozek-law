Legal Requirements — Know Your Case

The consular officer will evaluate each element below. Know exactly how your case satisfies each requirement before walking into the interview.

💼 L-1 Visa — Four Core Requirements (All Categories)
1
Qualifying Organization
The U.S. employer and foreign employer must have a qualifying relationship: parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch of the same employer. At least 50% common ownership or control is required for affiliates.
2
One Year of Qualifying Employment
You must have been employed continuously for at least 1 year within the 3 years immediately preceding the petition with the qualifying foreign organization, in a managerial, executive, or specialized knowledge capacity.
3
Qualifying Capacity Abroad
Your foreign employment must have been in a managerial, executive, or specialized knowledge capacity — the same categories as the U.S. position. You cannot qualify based on ordinary operational work.
4
Qualifying Capacity in the U.S.
The U.S. position must be in a managerial capacity (L-1A), executive capacity (L-1A), or specialized knowledge capacity (L-1B). The officer will scrutinize whether the U.S. role genuinely meets these definitions.
👑 L-1A: Manager or Executive — Key Definitions
M
Manager
Manages the organization, a department, subdivision, function, or component. Supervises and controls work of professional employees, managers, or supervisors — OR manages an essential function at a senior level. Has authority to hire/fire or recommend such action. Exercises discretion over day-to-day operations.
E
Executive
Directs the management of the organization or a major component or function. Establishes goals and policies. Exercises wide latitude in discretionary decision-making. Receives only general supervision from higher-level executives, a board of directors, or stockholders.
📊
Know Your Org Chart
For L-1A, the officer will probe whether your role is genuinely managerial or executive. Know who reports to you, their titles and qualifications, and what decisions you make. If your team is small, be prepared to explain why the role is still managerial.
🎯
Function Manager vs. People Manager
If you manage a function rather than people, be ready to explain what essential function you manage, your seniority level, and why professional or supervisory staff carry out routine operational work under your oversight.
🧠 L-1B: Specialized Knowledge — Key Definition
SK
Specialized Knowledge
Special knowledge of the petitioning organization's products, services, research, equipment, techniques, management, or other interests and its application in international markets — OR an advanced level of knowledge of the organization's processes and procedures. Must be genuinely proprietary or advanced — not generally available in the industry.
🧪
Proprietary vs. General Industry Knowledge
Officers frequently challenge L-1B cases by arguing the knowledge is available in the open market. Be prepared to explain specifically what makes your knowledge proprietary to this company — internal systems, confidential processes, unique methodologies, or company-specific technology that cannot be found elsewhere.
📖
Be Specific, Not General
Do not describe your role in generic industry terms. Use the company's proprietary names for systems, products, and processes. Describe training that is unique to the company, institutional knowledge built over years, and how your absence would specifically harm the U.S. operation.
🏗️ New Office L-1 — Additional Requirements
1
Physical Premises
The U.S. entity must have secured physical premises where the new office will operate before the visa is issued. A signed lease or purchase agreement is required.
2
Will Employ in Qualifying Capacity
For new office L-1A, the officer will assess whether the U.S. operation will, within one year, support a managerial or executive position. A business plan demonstrating realistic growth is critical.
3
One-Year Initial Period
New office L-1 is issued initially for 1 year only (vs. 3 years for established offices). Extension requires evidence that the office is doing business and the qualifying capacity is being exercised.

⚠ Nonimmigrant Intent — Critical for L-1

The L-1 is a nonimmigrant visa. You must demonstrate that you intend to depart the United States when your authorized status ends, unless you otherwise adjust status lawfully.

🚨
The officer is watching for immigrant intent. Unlike the E-1/E-2, L-1 holders CAN have "dual intent" — they may simultaneously pursue permanent residence (EB-1C petition) without prejudicing the L-1 visa. However, you must demonstrate that you have ties to your home country, a continuing role in the foreign entity, and that you are not abandoning your foreign life. Prepare these answers carefully.
🌍
Ties to Home Country
Be prepared to describe concrete ties: home or property owned or leased abroad, family members who remain in the home country, continued ownership interest in the foreign entity, and other financial or professional connections. Specific ties are more persuasive than general statements.
📄
Dual Intent Is Permitted
L-1 visa holders are explicitly protected under INA 214(b) from denial based solely on a pending or intended immigrant petition. If an I-140 or EB-1C petition has been filed, you do not need to hide it. However, the visa officer may still probe the overall picture of your ties and plans.
🏢
Continuing Role in Foreign Entity
Confirm that you have a continuing relationship with the foreign entity. The L-1 requires that you work for the qualifying organization in the U.S. — meaning the foreign entity must still exist and have a qualifying relationship with the U.S. entity throughout your authorized stay.
🚫
Never Misrepresent
Never lie about your intent, your employment history, or the corporate structure. Misrepresentation to a consular officer is a permanent ground of inadmissibility under INA 212(a)(6)(C). If a question concerns a sensitive area, discuss it with your attorney before the interview.

Interview Tips & Strategy

From the desk of Tolga Ozek, Esq. — what to know before you walk into the consulate for your L-1 interview.

⚠️
Important: Always consult with Ozek Law Firm before your consular interview. Call +1 (202) 854-8545 with any questions.
📊
Know the Corporate Structure Cold
The L-1 lives or dies on the corporate relationship. Know the ownership percentages, the type of relationship (parent, subsidiary, affiliate, branch), and how it is documented. Be able to draw the corporate structure from memory — name each entity, its jurisdiction of incorporation, and who owns what.
🗣️
Answer Only What Is Asked
Do not volunteer information beyond what the question requires. Keep answers concise and direct. Long, unprompted answers can introduce new issues and extend the interview into areas that cause problems.
👔
Dress for Business
You are representing yourself as a corporate manager, executive, or specialist. Dress accordingly: business professional or business formal. The consular officer makes quick judgments. Present yourself as the senior professional your petition describes.
🚫
Never Misrepresent
Misrepresentation to a consular officer is a permanent ground of inadmissibility under INA 212(a)(6)(C). If any aspect of your employment history, the corporate structure, or your qualifications is complex or sensitive, discuss the correct answer with your attorney before the interview.
⏰
Arrive No More Than 30 Minutes Early
Most consulates will not admit you more than 30 minutes before your appointment. Plan your route in advance. Allow for transportation delays. Being late can result in your interview slot being forfeited.
📋
Know the Petition Package
Study the table of contents of your petition package before the interview. Know where the corporate documents are, where the employment evidence is, and where your job description is. If the officer asks for a specific document, find it within seconds.
💻
No Laptops or Large Electronics
Most consulates do not permit laptops, tablets, or large backpacks inside. They will be confiscated at security. Bring only your documents organized in a folder, your photo ID, and your MRV fee receipt.
✍️
Write Everything Down After
The moment you leave the consulate, write down every question asked and every answer you gave — especially anything unexpected. If the officer expressed any concern or requested additional evidence, note it precisely and contact Ozek Law Firm the same day.
What to Expect at the Consulate
1
Security
Airport-style X-ray. No laptops or backpacks. Arrive no more than 30 minutes early.
2
Pre-Interview Check
Documents verified, sorted by visa type. Ask staff if unsure where to go.
3
The Interview
L-1 interviews typically last 10-30 minutes. Strong packages often result in shorter interviews.
4
Decision
Approval, 221(g) administrative processing, or denial. A 221(g) is NOT a denial.
5
221(g) — Do Not Panic
Request for additional documents or administrative processing. Call Ozek Law Firm immediately: +1 (202) 854-8545.
