Interview Tips & Guidance

From the desk of Tolga Ozek, LL.M., Esq. — what every naturalization applicant must know before walking in.

⚠️
Important: Always consult with Ozek Law Firm before your naturalization interview. Call +1 (202) 854-8545 or email info@ozeklaw.com.
🤝
Bring Your Attorney — Or Know Your Rights Alone
An attorney is not required but is strongly recommended depending on your case facts. If your attorney accompanies you, they observe — they cannot answer for you. If they will appear by phone, tell the officer before entering the room. Confirm this arrangement with Ozek Law Firm in advance.
📝
Answer Only What Is Asked
Do not volunteer information beyond what the officer requests. Keep answers short and direct. A "yes" or "no" is often the complete answer. Over-explaining can open doors to more questions and unintended complications.
📚
Know Your N-400 Inside and Out
Almost every question the officer asks comes directly from your N-400 application. Re-read it before the interview. If any information has changed or was filed incorrectly, notify Ozek Law Firm immediately — the interview is when corrections are made, not after.
🚫
Never Lie to a Federal Officer
Misrepresentation to USCIS is a federal crime and a permanent bar to naturalization. Even if you made a mistake on the N-400, the answer is to correct it honestly — not to double down on the error. USCIS runs extensive background checks.
💬
On the Civics Test: Guess If Uncertain
Unlike general interview questions, if you are unsure of a civics answer, it is better to try your best guess than to say "I don't know." You might actually remember the correct answer once you begin speaking. You need 12 of 20 — that is 60%.
📄
You May Refer to Documents — Except During Civics
If you need to check a document to answer a question (for example, a social security number you have not memorized), it is acceptable to say "I need to check my documents." However, you may NOT look at any notes or aids during the civics oral examination.
⏰
Arrive Early & Dressed Professionally
Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your appointment. Security lines at USCIS offices can be long. Dress in business casual or formal attire — first impressions at a federal office matter. Being late can result in your interview being marked as abandoned.
🇹🇷
Turkish-Origin Clients: Nüfus Documents
Turkish birth registration documents (nüfus kayıt örneği) require specific certified translations and careful preparation. Inform Ozek Law Firm of your citizenship registration status well in advance so correct documentation guidance can be provided.
What Happens at the Naturalization Interview?
1
Check-In & Oath
Present your notice and ID. You will be sworn in — everything said from that point must be truthful.
2
English Test
Reading and writing tests: you read one of three sentences correctly and write one of three correctly. Speaking is assessed throughout.
3
Civics Test
Officer asks up to 20 questions from the 128-question civics bank. Answer 12 correctly to pass. No notes allowed.
4
N-400 Review
Officer reviews your application. Questions cover your background, travel history, criminal record (if any), and attachments.
5
Decision
Approval (oath ceremony scheduled), continuation, or denial. Contact Ozek Law Firm immediately for anything other than approval.
